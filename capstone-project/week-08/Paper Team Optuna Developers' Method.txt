Paper: Team Optuna Developers' Method for Black-Box Optimization Challenge 2020
Of the six papers, I focused on Optuna as it represents a low-compute, evaluation-budget-constrained approach — the constraint that matters most when working on a single laptop with one submission per week. NVIDIA's entry names GPU-accelerated exhaustive search in its title; Huawei's HEBO runs an evolutionary acquisition maximiser across the full candidate pool every iteration. Optuna and JetBrains are the two entries that competed on equal terms with my setup, finishing 5th and 3rd respectively.
1. Core methodology
Optuna extends TuRBO, which fits a local GP inside a trust region around the current best point, expanding or contracting based on consecutive successes or failures. Their three additions target real failure modes: multi-kernel Thompson sampling hedges against not knowing the function's smoothness by sampling candidates across several GP kernels simultaneously; a stagnation-driven restart fires immediately when an entire batch returns identical values rather than waiting for standard trust-region shrinkage; and Sobol sequence initialisation provides better space-filling uniformity than Latin hypercube at small sample counts. Traditional BO fixes one kernel and one restart criterion — Optuna adapts to whatever shape the objective actually has.
2. Advantages
Multi-kernel selection means the surrogate doesn't degrade when your prior about smoothness is wrong. The stagnation restart addresses a genuine blind spot in standard TuRBO — one my own pipeline has hit repeatedly. F1 returned near-zero values for seven consecutive weeks; Optuna's trigger would have fired after the first flat batch rather than requiring manual escalation each week. F4's injection strategy failed silently for five weeks before I abandoned it manually. Both are textbook stagnation cases that a formal algorithmic trigger would have caught earlier.
3. Limitations
Sampling across multiple kernels multiplies GP fitting cost per iteration. The stagnation restart also discards all trust-region state, risking abandonment of a slowly-improving region — F4 illustrates this risk exactly, where the inject may have been working but too slowly to register above the noise floor within the trigger window. The method also assumes synchronous batch evaluation, which doesn't hold in real-world asynchronous deployments.
4. Real-world applications
Any domain where evaluation is expensive and landscape shape is unknown: neural architecture search, drug discovery, materials simulation, clinical trial dosing. Mixed-variable support makes it practical for AutoML pipelines combining continuous, integer and categorical hyperparameters across complex, irregular search spaces.
5. Recommendations — applied to my Week 8 capstone
I am applying all three Optuna strategies this week across my four most challenging functions. For F1 (2D, seven weeks near-zero): full stagnation restart — trust region wiped, sigma escalated to 0.40, and the 2,000 random explore candidates replaced with Sobol sequences across the full [0,1]² space for provably better coverage. I am also adding an RBF kernel alongside Matérn 5/2, since EI becomes numerically unreliable when the incumbent is near zero and the smoothness assumption is almost certainly wrong after seven flat weeks. For F4 (4D, inject failed after five weeks): the same treatment — Sobol across [0,1]⁴ for the 3,500 explore candidates, plus multi-kernel hedging, since abandoning the inject means the landscape is effectively unknown again. For F2 and F3, which are in tight exploit mode around known best points, none of the three strategies apply — Gaussian sampling around the best point is correct, Matérn is sufficient, and introducing Sobol or a restart would only add noise to a working setup.
For peers: start with Sobol initialisation on any stuck function — it is a one-line change via scipy.stats.qmc.Sobol and immediately improves candidate quality. Then formalise your stagnation criterion before you need it. Manual escalation after several disappointing weeks is exactly what Optuna replaces with a clean algorithmic rule.
